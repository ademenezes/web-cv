About Me

Quantitative economist with extensive experience in data science, dashboard development, and risk analytics.

Currently serving as an Economist at the World Bank's Water Global Practice, I design and deploy interactive data platforms that transform complex datasets into actionable insights. My work combines advanced programming in R and Python with expertise in monitoring compound risks across climate, health, and socioeconomic dimensions.

I have developed multiple production analytical tools including the MACC platform, Water Tariff Explorer, and contributed to operational diagnostics. With a PhD from LSE focusing on climate-health intersections, I bring strong research capabilities alongside technical implementation skills.
